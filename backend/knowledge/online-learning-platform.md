## CHATBOT BEHAVIOR — Greetings and Welcome

**IMPORTANT: When the user greets you**, you MUST respond warmly. Do NOT reply with "Sorry, I can only help with questions related to this learning platform."

**Greeting phrases** include (any variation, in any language or casual form): hello, hi, hey, good morning, good afternoon, good evening, hi there, hey there, howdy, greetings, what's up, how are you, hello there, hi again, good day.

**When the user says any greeting (e.g. "hello", "hi", "hey"):**
- Respond with a friendly welcome message.
- Introduce yourself briefly as the platform's AI assistant.
- Invite them to ask about the platform: courses, enrollment, lessons, quizzes, progress, certificates, or troubleshooting.
- Keep the reply concise (2–4 sentences) and warm.

**Example response to "hello" or "hi":**  
"Hello! Nice to meet you. I'm your AI assistant for the Online Learning Platform. I can help you with courses, enrollment, lessons, quizzes, certificates, or any questions about how the platform works. What would you like to know?"

Use the knowledge in this document to answer follow-up questions. For non-greeting messages that are off-topic (e.g. general knowledge, other subjects), you may say you focus on the learning platform and offer to help with that.

## Platform Overview

This is an Online Learning Platform designed for students, instructors, and administrators. The platform provides comprehensive course management, interactive lessons, quizzes, progress tracking, and certification features.

## User Roles

### Students
Students can:
- Browse and enroll in courses
- Access course lessons and materials
- Take quizzes to test their knowledge
- Track their learning progress
- Download certificates upon course completion
- View their dashboard with enrolled courses

### Instructors
Instructors can:
- Create and manage courses
- Add lessons to courses (videos, PDFs, external links)
- Create quizzes with multiple choice questions
- View analytics and student progress
- Manage course content and materials

### Administrators
Administrators can:
- Manage all users (students, instructors, admins)
- View platform-wide analytics
- Manage all courses and content
- Issue certificates to students
- Control platform settings including chatbot visibility

## Enrollment Process

### How to Enroll in a Course (Students)

1. **Browse Courses**: Navigate to the Courses page to see all available published courses
2. **View Course Details**: Click on a course to see its description, lessons, and quizzes
3. **Enroll**: Click the "Enroll" button on the course details page
4. **Access Course**: Once enrolled, the course appears in your Student Dashboard
5. **Start Learning**: Access lessons and quizzes from your enrolled courses

### Enrollment Status

- **Active**: Student is currently enrolled and learning
- **Completed**: Student has finished all lessons and quizzes
- **Inactive**: Enrollment is paused or cancelled

## Lessons

### How Lessons Work

1. **Lesson Types**:
   - Video lessons with progress tracking
   - PDF documents
   - External URL links
   - Text-based content

2. **Accessing Lessons**:
   - Navigate to your enrolled course
   - Click on a lesson to open it
   - For videos: Watch and your progress is automatically saved
   - For PDFs: Download or view in browser
   - For external links: Click to open in new tab

3. **Lesson Completion**:
   - Lessons are marked as complete when you finish viewing them
   - Video progress is tracked (you can resume from where you left off)
   - Completed lessons are shown with a checkmark

### Video Progress Tracking

- Your video watch progress is automatically saved
- You can pause and resume videos from where you left off
- Progress is synced across devices when logged in

## Quizzes

### How Quizzes Work

1. **Taking a Quiz**:
   - Navigate to the quiz from your course
   - Click "Take Quiz" to start
   - Answer all questions (multiple choice)
   - Submit your answers when complete

2. **Quiz Results**:
   - You'll see your score immediately after submission
   - Passing score is typically 70% or higher (varies by course)
   - You can review which answers were correct/incorrect
   - Quiz attempts are saved in your progress

3. **Quiz Types**:
   - Multiple choice questions
   - Single correct answer per question
   - Questions may have multiple correct answers (select all that apply)

### Quiz Attempts

- Each quiz can be taken multiple times
- Your best score is typically used for course completion
- All attempts are recorded in your quiz history
- Passing a quiz contributes to course completion

2. **Quiz Progress**:
   - Passing quizzes contributes to completion

## Certificates

### How Certificates Work

1. **Eligibility**:
   - Complete all lessons in a course
   - Pass all quizzes
   - Enrollment status must be "Completed"

2. **Issuance**:
   - Admin issues certificates to eligible students
   - Each certificate has a unique verification code

3. **Verification**:
   - Certificates can be verified using the verification code
   - Download certificate as PDF
   - Share certificate with employers or on LinkedIn

## Common Issues and Solutions

### Video Not Working

**Possible Causes:**
1. Video URL is invalid or expired
2. Browser compatibility issues
3. Network connectivity problems
4. Video platform restrictions

**Solutions:**
1. Check if video URL is correct and accessible
2. Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge)
3. Check your internet connection
4. Clear browser cache and cookies
5. Contact instructor if video link needs updating

### Quiz Not Submitting

**Possible Causes:**
1. Not all questions answered
2. Network connectivity issues
3. Browser session expired

**Solutions:**
1. Ensure all questions have answers selected
2. Check internet connection
3. Refresh page and try again
4. Log out and log back in if session expired

### Progress Not Updating

**Possible Causes:**
1. Lesson not fully completed
2. Quiz not passed
3. Browser cache issues

**Solutions:**
1. Ensure you've watched entire video or completed lesson
2. Pass the quiz with required score
3. Refresh the page
4. Clear browser cache

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### For Students

**Q: How do I find my enrolled courses?**  
A: After logging in, go to your Student Dashboard. All enrolled courses are listed there with progress and quick links to continue learning.

**Q: Can I unenroll from a course?**  
A: Enrollment options depend on platform settings. Check the course details page or contact the instructor or administrator for your organization.

**Q: Why is my video progress not saving?**  
A: Make sure you are logged in and have a stable connection. Progress is saved automatically; try refreshing the page or completing a bit more of the video and waiting a few seconds.

**Q: What happens if I fail a quiz?**  
A: You can usually retake quizzes. Your attempts are recorded and often the best score counts toward completion. Check the quiz rules on the course page.

**Q: How do I get my certificate?**  
A: Complete all lessons and pass all quizzes. Once your enrollment shows as Completed, an administrator can issue your certificate. You can then download it from the Certificates area.

**Q: Can I use the platform on my phone?**  
A: The platform is web-based and works in mobile browsers. For the best experience, use a modern browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge) and a stable internet connection.

**Q: Who do I contact if I have technical issues?**  
A: Use the AI Assistant (this chatbot) for quick answers, or contact your platform administrator or instructor for account- or course-specific help.

### For Instructors

**Q: How do I make my course visible to students?**  
A: In course editing, turn on the "Publish" toggle and save. Only published courses appear in the public course list.

**Q: Can I reorder lessons?**  
A: Yes. Use the lesson order or position field when adding or editing lessons. Lower numbers usually appear first.

**Q: How do I set the passing score for a quiz?**  
A: When creating or editing a quiz, set the "Passing Score" (as a percentage). Students must reach this score to pass.

**Q: Can students see quiz answers after submitting?**  
A: This depends on platform configuration. Typically, students can see their score and sometimes which answers were correct after submission.

**Q: How do I add a video lesson?**  
A: Add a lesson, choose or use the default type for video, and paste the video URL (e.g. from YouTube or Vimeo) in the Video URL field. Save the lesson.

### For Administrators

**Q: How do I enable or disable the AI chatbot?**  
A: Go to Admin Settings and use the "Chatbot visibility" or similar option to turn the chatbot on or off for all users.

**Q: How do I issue a certificate?**  
A: In the admin area, find the Certificates or Students section, locate the student who has completed the course, and use the option to issue or generate the certificate.

**Q: Can I delete a course?**  
A: Administrative options usually allow deleting or archiving courses. This may affect enrollments and progress; check your admin documentation or backup data first.

## Glossary

- **Enrollment**: Registering for a course as a student. Gives access to lessons and quizzes.
- **Course**: A structured set of lessons and quizzes on a topic, created by an instructor.
- **Lesson**: A single unit of content (video, PDF, link, or text) within a course.
- **Quiz**: A set of questions (usually multiple choice) used to assess understanding.
- **Passing score**: The minimum percentage required to pass a quiz (e.g. 70%).
- **Progress**: How much of a course a student has completed (lessons and quizzes).
- **Certificate**: A document issued when a student completes a course, often with a verification code.
- **Dashboard**: The main page after login showing courses, progress, and shortcuts.
- **Publish**: Making a course visible and enrollable by students.
- **Instructor**: User who creates and manages courses.
- **Student**: User who enrolls in courses and completes lessons and quizzes.
- **Admin**: User who manages the platform, users, and settings.

## Detailed Student Workflows

### First-Time Student: From Sign-Up to First Lesson

1. Create an account or log in.
2. Open the Courses page and browse available courses.
3. Click a course to view description, lessons, and quizzes.
4. Click "Enroll" to enroll in the course.
5. Go to the Student Dashboard and open the enrolled course.
6. Click the first lesson to open it.
7. For videos, watch until the end (or your progress is saved when you leave).
8. Return to the course view and start the next lesson when ready.
9. When you reach a quiz, click "Take Quiz", answer all questions, and submit.
10. If you pass, continue; if not, retake if allowed and try again.
11. After completing all lessons and passing all quizzes, your status becomes Completed and you may be eligible for a certificate.

### Resuming Where You Left Off

1. Log in and open the Student Dashboard.
2. Find your enrolled course and check the progress bar.
3. Click "Continue" or open the course.
4. The next incomplete lesson or quiz is typically highlighted or listed first.
5. For video lessons, the player usually resumes from the last saved position.

### Requesting or Downloading a Certificate

1. Ensure the course is fully completed (all lessons and quizzes done, status "Completed").
2. Go to the Certificates section (often under Profile, Dashboard, or a top menu).
3. If your organization requires admin issuance, wait until the certificate is issued.
4. When available, click Download or View and save or print the PDF.
5. Use the verification code on the certificate if someone needs to verify it.

## Detailed Instructor Workflows

### Creating a Course from Scratch

1. Log in as an instructor and go to Instructor Dashboard → Courses.
2. Click "Create New Course".
3. Enter course title and short description.
4. Add full description, category, and difficulty.
5. Upload or set a thumbnail if desired.
6. Save as draft, add lessons and quizzes, then turn on "Publish" when ready.
7. Share the course link or direct students to the Courses page.

### Adding a Video Lesson

1. Open the course and go to the Lessons section.
2. Click "Add Lesson".
3. Enter a lesson title (e.g. "Introduction to Topic").
4. Paste the video URL (YouTube, Vimeo, or other supported source) in the Video URL field.
5. Set lesson order and add a short description if needed.
6. Save. Students will see the lesson and their watch progress will be tracked.

### Creating a Multiple-Choice Quiz

1. Open the course and go to the Quizzes section.
2. Click "Create Quiz".
3. Enter quiz title and description.
4. Set passing score (e.g. 70).
5. Add questions via "Add Question".
6. For each question: enter text, add options, mark the correct one(s), save.
7. Save the quiz. It will appear in the course for students to take.

## Troubleshooting Scenarios (Step-by-Step)

### Scenario: "I enrolled but the course doesn’t show on my dashboard"

1. Log out and log back in.
2. Hard-refresh the page (e.g. Ctrl+F5 or Cmd+Shift+R).
3. Confirm you are on the correct account (check your name/email in the header).
4. Check the exact URL or link you used to enroll.
5. If it still does not appear, contact support with your account email and course name.

### Scenario: "The video won’t play or keeps buffering"

1. Pause and wait a few seconds; then press play again.
2. Lower the video quality if the player has a quality option.
3. Close other tabs or apps that use bandwidth.
4. Try another browser or device.
5. Check if the same video plays on the original site (e.g. YouTube).
6. If it still fails, report the course and lesson name to the instructor or admin.

### Scenario: "I submitted the quiz but it says I didn’t answer all questions"

1. Before submitting, scroll through the quiz and ensure every question has a selected option.
2. Some quizzes require a minimum number of options; check the instructions.
3. Submit again after selecting all required answers.
4. If the problem continues, take a screenshot and contact the instructor or support.

### Scenario: "My certificate isn’t showing even though I completed the course"

1. Confirm the course status is "Completed" (all lessons and quizzes done).
2. Check the Certificates or Achievements section in your account.
3. Some platforms require an admin to issue the certificate; allow some time or contact the admin.
4. Ensure you are logged into the correct account.

### Scenario: "I am an instructor and my new lesson doesn’t appear"

1. Save the lesson and refresh the course page.
2. Check that the lesson is not in "Draft" or "Hidden" state if such options exist.
3. Clear your browser cache and try again.
4. Confirm you are editing the correct course.

### Scenario: "The AI chatbot is not responding or shows an error"

1. Check your internet connection.
2. Refresh the page and try a short message like "hello" or "hi".
3. If it still fails, the service may be temporarily unavailable; try again later.
4. For repeated errors, report to your administrator.

## Instructor Tips for Better Courses

1. **Structure**: Use clear lesson order and descriptive titles so students know what to expect.
2. **Video length**: Shorter segments (e.g. 5–15 minutes) often work better than very long videos.
3. **Quiz alignment**: Match quiz questions to the lessons so progress reflects real learning.
4. **Passing score**: Set a fair passing score (e.g. 70–80%) and allow retakes if appropriate.
5. **Descriptions**: Write a clear course and lesson description to help students choose and follow along.
6. **Updates**: When you change or remove content, inform enrolled students if possible.
7. **Support**: Tell students how to contact you (e.g. chat, email) for course-specific questions.

1. **Users**: Regularly review roles (student, instructor, admin) and disable or remove unused accounts as per policy.

5. **Backups**: Ensure course content, user data, and progress are backed up according to your organization’s rules.
6. **Feedback**: Track common support questions and consider adding answers to the knowledge base or FAQ.
